Two numbers that matter

Net revenue is what marketplaces pay out after commission — the money that actually lands. Contribution margin is what's left after both commission and the cost of goods. Every downstream chart here reconciles back to these.

Channel-level Contribution

Marketplace vs Own-store

Volume ≠ profit

Marketplaces move the highest volume — but the own-store, with zero commission, delivers a materially higher contribution margin per baht sold.

The cannibalization question sits underneath this: which own-store customers would still buy direct if the marketplace listing didn't exist?

Commission rates: marketplaces 12–15%, own-store 0%.

Promotion Impact — AOV Comparison

Promo effect

Bigger baskets, not just more baskets

Promotion days pull in materially higher AOV than non-promotion days — the discount attracts customers who bundle more items per cart, not just customers who would have bought anyway.

The counterweight: promo orders carry a lower per-unit margin, so the AOV lift and margin drag need to be read together to score the campaign.
